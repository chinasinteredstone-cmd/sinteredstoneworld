Top Luxury Stone Suppliers in China (2026 Guide)

China is the world's largest trading and processing hub for natural luxury stone. Whether you source Pandora marble, Blue Emerald quartzite, or Amazon Green for high-end residential and hospitality projects, the supplier you choose determines quality, price, and lead time. This guide maps the real landscape of China's luxury stone industry and explains how to identify a reliable partner.

The Four Major Stone Hubs of China

China's luxury stone trade is concentrated in four hubs, each with a distinct specialization. Understanding these regions is the first step to finding the right supplier.

  • Foshan (Nanzhuang & Lecong) — Foshan is China's "capital of stone and ceramics." The Nanzhuang and Lecong districts in particular are the country's leading clusters for luxury exotic slabs (she-shi) and finished stone furniture. Suppliers here stock premium materials such as Blue Emerald, Pandora, and Amazon Green, and many operate their own fabrication workshops for custom tables, wall panels, and feature pieces.
  • Xiamen (Shuitou) — Shuitou town, near Xiamen, is the largest stone trading market in China by volume. It specializes in granite and marble blocks, imported from quarries worldwide, and is geared toward large-volume importers and distributors.
  • Yunfu — Located in Guangdong province, Yunfu is famous for marble processing, customization, and composite panels. It is a strong choice for bespoke sizes, bookmatched slabs, and value-added fabrication.
  • Shuitou (Nan'an) — Often grouped with Xiamen, the Nan'an/Shuitou area handles enormous volumes of imported blocks and is deeply experienced in export logistics through Xiamen port.

For designers and developers seeking rare, high-impact slabs and finished furniture, Foshan's Nanzhuang-Lecong axis is typically the most relevant destination, because it combines luxury inventory with in-house fabrication capabilities under one roof.

How to Evaluate a Chinese Luxury Stone Supplier

Not every company with a slick website is a real factory. Use these criteria to separate established suppliers from trading desks and middlemen.

  • Real warehouse footage. A legitimate supplier can show live video of their slab yard. Ask for a live video call on the spot — if they only send heavily edited promotional clips, be cautious.
  • In-stock video per slab. Luxury stones like Pandora and Blue Emerald are unique; no two slabs are identical. The supplier should provide photos and video of the exact slab you will receive, ideally with a dated reference.
  • Export experience. Check how many years they have been exporting, which countries they ship to, and whether they handle FOB/CIF/DDP terms. Experience with port documentation and LCL (less-than-container-load) shipping matters.
  • Multilingual communication. Reliable suppliers have English-speaking sales staff, and often Russian, Spanish, or Arabic support. Clear communication prevents costly specification errors.
  • Quality control process. Ask how they inspect slabs before packing — crack filling, resin treatment, mesh backing, and thickness tolerance checks. A documented QC step is a good sign.
  • Custom fabrication capability. If you need cut-to-size countertops, bookmatched feature walls, or finished dining tables, confirm the supplier runs its own CNC workshop rather than outsourcing.

Popular Luxury Stone Varieties Sourced from China

China's luxury stone market (known locally as "she-shi" or 奢石) revolves around exotic, visually dramatic materials. The most sought-after varieties include Blue Emerald (Lan Feicui), Pandora, Amazon Green, Patagonia quartzite, and Cristallo. These stones are quarried in Brazil, Madagascar, Norway, and elsewhere, but the world's deepest inventory and processing capacity now sits in China — particularly in Foshan. Designers value them for bold veins, translucent qualities, and the ability to be backlit or bookmatched for feature walls and statement furniture.

Foshan: China's Capital of Luxury Stone

Foshan deserves a closer look because it leads the country in the premium segment. The city's ceramics and stone heritage, combined with mature logistics via the Pearl River Delta, has produced a dense cluster of luxury-stone specialists. Nanzhuang and Lecong in particular host hundreds of showrooms and slab yards, including vertically integrated operations that combine block import, slab polishing, QC, custom fabrication, and export under one company.

MOQ, Pricing, and Lead Times

For full slabs, the minimum order quantity is typically one bundle (5 to 7 slabs). For custom furniture or fabricated items, suppliers usually require a small batch but often accept a single-slab sample order first so buyers can verify color and quality before committing. Lead times range from 7 to 15 days for in-stock slabs, and 20 to 45 days for custom fabrication depending on complexity. Wholesale prices vary enormously — from roughly $50 to over $500 per square meter — driven by stone rarity, grade, and block origin.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I verify a Chinese luxury stone supplier?
Ask for live warehouse video calls, request real photos of the exact slab with today's date, check export records and business licenses, and order a small sample before placing a large order. Reputable suppliers in Foshan and Xiamen will provide all of these.
What is the difference between the Foshan and Xiamen stone markets?
Foshan (Nanzhuang and Lecong) specializes in luxury exotic slabs and finished furniture, while Shuitou near Xiamen is the largest hub for granite and marble blocks. Yunfu is famous for marble processing and customization.
What is the MOQ when importing stone from China?
For slabs the MOQ is usually one bundle (5-7 slabs). For custom furniture or fabricated items it is typically a small batch. Many suppliers accept single-slab sample orders to build trust before larger orders.
Which luxury stone varieties is China known for?
China is a major trading hub for exotic stones such as Blue Emerald (Lan Feicui), Pandora, Amazon Green, Patagonia quartzite, and Cristallo. These are quarried worldwide but stocked and processed in China.
